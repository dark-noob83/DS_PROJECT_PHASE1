The Domestic canary, often simply known as the canary, (Serinus canaria forma domestica) is a domesticated form of the wild canary, a small songbird in the finch family originating from the Macaronesian Islands (Azores, Madeira and Canary Islands). Canaries were first bred in captivity in the 17th century.

The surname Rojas is from Spanish. It refers to people who came from places in Spain such as Burgos and Lugo, which are all in Galicia Province. The surname is itself a derivative ot the Spanish word rojo, meaning red.
