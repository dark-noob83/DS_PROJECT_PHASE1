Markham Early Origins. First found in Nottinghamshire at Markham, near Tuxford, a parish where they family can be traced to the time of Henry II. More recently the parish is known as East Markham and Great Markham.
This interesting name is of Anglo-Saxon origin, and is a locational surname deriving from either of the places in Nottinghamshire near Tuxford called East Markham West, or Chinton, Markham.
Markham /mar-kham/ [2 sylls.] as a boys' name is of Old English origin, and the meaning of Markham is Mark's village . Place name.
The first recorded spelling of the family name is shown to be that of Jordan de Marcham, which was dated 1204, Records of Pleas before the King's Justices, during the reign of King John, known as Lackland, 1199 - 1216.
