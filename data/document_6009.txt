The maximum Federal Pell Grant was $5,645 in the 2013-2014 academic year and $5,730 in 2014-2015. The maximum Federal Pell Grant is expected to increase to $5,775 in 2015-2016. More than 95 percent (95.9%) of Federal Pell Grant recipients in 2011-2012 had family adjusted gross income (AGI) under $60,000. Less than one percent of Federal Pell Grant recipients had family AGI of $100,000 or more.
The maximum award of a Pell Grant varies from one academic year to the next. For the 2013-2014 academic year, for example, the most money you could receive from a Pell Grant was $5,645. That amount will increase to $5,775 for the 2015-2016 academic year. Not all students receive the maximum award. According to the American Association of State Colleges and Universities, the Federal Pell Grant program awarded over $33 billion in 2011 to 2012, and close to 10 million undergraduates received awards. But the grant is highly competitive, and only those who meet the criteria for financial need are eligible.
