In England and Ireland, an apothecary is defined as a person who is entrusted to both prescribe medicine and sell it. An example of an apothecary is Joseph Proust. An apothecary is defined as the person in a pharmacy who is trusted to distribute medicine responsibly and accurately. An example of an apothecary is a pharmacist or druggist.
Apothecary is a term that refers to an individual who prepares and sells drugs and medicines. The term was used often in medieval Europe, but is seldom used in modern times.
