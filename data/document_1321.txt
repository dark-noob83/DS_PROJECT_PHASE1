Afelia (Marinated Pork in red wine). Afelia is a traditional Cypriot dish of small pieces of fried pork meat cooked in red dry wine and coriande r. It is usually accompanied by bulgur pilaf. The origin of the word comes from ancient Greek and they were called ovelia = avelia. The word ovelias means cooked meat (usually lamb) on the spit. My mother used to cook this dish for us quite often. I spiced it up a bit, giving my own twist to this dish.
