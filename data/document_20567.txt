The name Lynx comes from the Latin word lynx, which is derived from the Greek word “leukos” which means white or bright light. Two of the four species of the lynx family are native to the United States, and the other two species can be found in Europe and Asia.
Lynx. A lynx is any of the four species within the Lynx genus of medium-sized wildcats. The name lynx originated in Middle English via Latin from the Greek word λύγξ, derived from the Indo-European root *leuk-, meaning light, brightness, in reference to the luminescence of its reflective eyes.
