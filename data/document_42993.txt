PayScale.com reported that as of January 2014, jewelers with 5-9 years of experience made $28,662-$38,974 a year. Those with 10-19 years of experience earned $38,000-$58,958, while those with 20 years of experience or more earned $50,868-$91,975.
The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) predicted that jewelers and precious stone and metal workers, which includes jewelry repair technicians, would see a ten percent decline in employment from 2012-2022 (www.bls.gov). This will lead to the elimination of 3,200 jobs by 2022, the BLS noted.
According to the BLS, jewelers and precious stone and metal workers employed by jewelry, luggage and leather goods stores made an average salary of $42,760.
The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) reported that jewelers and precious stone and metal workers in general made an average annual salary of $38,840 in May 2012 (www.bls.gov). The bottom 10% made $19,600 a year or less, and the top 10% made $61,940 a year or more.
