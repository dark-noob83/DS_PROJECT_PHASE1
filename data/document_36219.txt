Belgium's government is a Constitutional Monarchy and Parliamentary Democracy with 150 members in its legislature. Executive elections are every 4 years. The percent of seats in Belgian parliament held by women is 2.2 times higher than the global average, 17% .
Belgium is a constitutional monarchy based on heredity. After political reforms in the 20th century, the monarch's role is now largely ceremonial and symbolic. The monarch's main political function is to appoint the prime minister following elections or the resignation of the government.
