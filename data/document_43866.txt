During a physical exam, your health care provider may find: 1  Numbness in the palm, thumb, index finger, middle finger, and thumb side of your ring finger. 2  Weak hand grip. 3  Tapping over the median nerve at your wrist may cause pain to shoot from your wrist to your hand (this is called the Tinel sign).
The median nerve gives feeling and movement to the palm side of your hand. This includes the palm side of the thumb, index, finger, middle finger, and ring finger. The area in your wrist where this nerve enters the hand is called the carpal tunnel. This tunnel is normally narrow. Any swelling can pinch the nerve and cause pain, numbness, tingling, or weakness.
The most common causes of hand or finger weakness are carpal tunnel syndrome, mononeuritis, and complex regional pain syndrome. Other possible causes, such as rheumatoid arthritis, are more rare.
