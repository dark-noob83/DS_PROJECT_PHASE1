In the US, a shot is 1.5 ounces and a small shot is 1 ounce of liquor for an alcoholic beverage such as whiskey. However, this varies internationally and there is no absolutely defined unit, and various sizes of glasses are used.
Other shot glass sizes are called short shot glasses or pony shots both of which are 1 ounce. Alcoholism experts define a standard drink as 12 ounces of beer, 1.5 ounces of 72-proof distilled spirits, or 5 ounces of wine, all of which contain approximately .54 ounces of alcohol.
Since a shot is typically a spirit with 40 percent alcohol, this makes the shot 1.5 oz. or 42.62 mL (though many establishments serve a standard drink of 1 oz). A double shot in North America may be either 2.5 or 3 fluid ounces.
