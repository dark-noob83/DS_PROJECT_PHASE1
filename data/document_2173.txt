A bachelor's degree in social work is the minimum education required to become an entry-level social worker. Bachelor's degrees usually take four years to complete and require both general education courses and courses specific to social work. The Academy of Certified Social Workers is a credential offered to current NASW members with an MSW degree. They must have two years of supervised experience in social work and recommendations from their supervisor and two peers. In addition to the MSW, 20 hours of continuing education is required.

Licensure. While many social work jobs require only an MSW and some require only a BSW, most social workers obtain a license, and all clinical social workers are required to be licensed. Licensing requirements vary by state. The exam fee is $260 for licensure at the clinical level, and $230 at the master's level. You will also need to pay an application fee to sit for the exam, and another fee to obtain the license. These additional fees also vary, depending on what state you are in.
