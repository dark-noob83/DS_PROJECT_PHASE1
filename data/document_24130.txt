How much does a director make per year? According to Indeed.com, the salary for a film director is $84,000 per year. A new movie director is able to earn upwards of $200,000 a year, while highly successful directors are able to earn $500,000 or millions per film in some cases.
Films, classified as low or high budget, shorts or documentaries, earn different pay. High-budget films have budgets more than $11 million. Directors working a week on a high-budget film earned a minimum of $16,508 in 2012, while a week on a short or documentary paid $11,791. When the film takes more than the week, directors on high-budget productions earned $4,127 daily pay. Short and documentary film directors took home $2,948 for a day of work in 2012. The Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) reported in May 2011 that film directors earned an annual mean wage of $115,920.
