A bank's Routing Transit Number (RTN) is a 9-digit code used to identify your bank on checks, direct deposit, bill pay, and in other transactions. You need to know your routing number to connect online accounts to your bank account, set up direct deposit with your employer, and in many other financial situations.

Your bank routing number is the first nine-digit number on the bottom of each check. This number, often referred to as a routing transit number, identifies the financial institution that you bank with.The American Bankers Association assigns each financial institution in the United States one of these codes.
