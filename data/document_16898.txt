Acute pharyngitis is a painful inflammation of the throat (pharynx). Most often caused by a viral infection, it can also be caused by bacteria such as streptococci, M. pneumoniae, Neisseria gonorrhea, chlamydia, and corynebacterium haemolyticus. Viral pharyngitis is often associated with the common cold or influenza.
