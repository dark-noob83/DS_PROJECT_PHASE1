Gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH), also known as luteinizing hormone-releasing hormone (LHRH) and luliberin, as well as gonadorelin (INN), is a trophic peptide hormone responsible for the release of follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH) from the anterior pituitary.
GnRH functions as a chemical messenger that is sent from the brain to the genitals in a teenager. When GnRH is produced, it signals for the sexual organs to produce FSH (follicle stimulating hormone), and LH (luteinizing hormone). GnRH is a hormone that is produced in the hypothalamus region of the brain. Once produced, GnRH moves through the bloodstream to the pituitary gland. There, it binds to certain receptors, which signals the pituitary gland to create two more hormones: LH and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH).
