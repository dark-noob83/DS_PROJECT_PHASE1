Beta-carotene is a precursor for vitamin A. The body needs to convert it to retinol or vitamin A for use. Beta-carotene is found naturally in mostly orange and dark green plant foods, such as carrots, sweet potatoes, mangos, and kale.

Vitamin A is a fat-soluble vitamin that is naturally present in many foods. Vitamin A is important for normal vision, the immune system, and reproduction. Vitamin A also helps the heart, lungs, kidneys, and other organs work properly.
The foremost answer to the question what is vitamin A good for, is to improve vision. Therefore, lack of vitamin A in one's diet can lead to poor vision. The diseases caused by lack of a particular vitamin / nutrient in the diet are known as deficiency diseases.
What is Vitamin A Good for. Vitamins are essential nutrients required to carry out most of the important functions in the body. Vitamins are divided into 6 types, viz. Vitamin A, B, C, D, E and K. Among them, vitamins A, D, E and K are fat-soluble ones while vitamins B and C are water-soluble vitamins. Vitamins are very essential for growth, cell division, proper functioning of the cell, etc.
Vitamin A is essential for good health. It promotes growth, the immune system, reproduction, and vision. However, recent research suggests that too much vitamin A, particularly in the form of retinol, may be bad for your bones.
What You Eat Matters. Eating plenty of fruits and vegetables rich in antioxidants is good for your whole body, including your skin. Antioxidants such as beta-carotene and vitamins C, E, and A can curb the damage caused by unstable molecules known as free radicals. These molecules can harm skin cells and cause signs of aging.
