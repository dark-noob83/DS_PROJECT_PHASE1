Future Shock is a book written by the futurist Alvin Toffler in 1970. In the book, Toffler defines the term  future shock  as a certain psychological state of individuals and entire societies. His shortest definition for the term is a personal perception of too much change in too short a period of time. The book, which became an international bestseller, grew out of an article The Future as a Way of Life in Horizon magazine, Summer 1965 issue.
Alvin Toffler was born in 1928 in New York City, the son of Rose (Albaum) and Sam Toffler. His family was Jewish. He met his future wife, Adelaide Elizabeth Farrell (now known as Heidi Toffler), at New York University where he was an English major and she was starting a graduate course in linguistics. Toffler is a former associate editor of Fortune magazine. In his early works he focused on technology and its impact through effects like information overload. He moved on to examining the reaction to changes in society.
