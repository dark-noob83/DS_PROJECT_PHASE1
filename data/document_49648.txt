CHICAGO, March 11— William Wrigley, chief executive of the Wm. Wrigley Jr. Company and the heir of the chewing gum family that once owned the Chicago Cubs baseball team, died Monday after a brief bout of pneumonia. He was 66.
In 1892, William Wrigley, Jr. , the company's founder, began packaging chewing gum with each can of baking powder. The chewing gum eventually became more popular than the baking powder and Wrigley's reoriented the company to produce the gum.
