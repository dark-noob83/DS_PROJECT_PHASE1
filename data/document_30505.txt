There are two types of bone marrow; red bone marrow and yellow bone marrow. At birth, a man’s bone marrow is all red. As he ages, more of the red bone marrow is converted to yellow. Red bone marrow can be found in flat bones such as the hip bone, breast bone, skull, ribs, and shoulder blades.
The two types of bone marrow are red marrow (Latin: medulla ossium rubra), which consists mainly of hematopoietic tissue, and yellow marrow (Latin: medulla ossium flava), which is mainly made up of fat cells.
