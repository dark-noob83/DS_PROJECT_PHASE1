A 2010 salary survey performed by the American Society of Radiologic Technologists provides insight into the effect of a bachelor's degree on pay. For technologists across all disciplines, the average wage with an associate degree was $58,836 a year. The wage for those with a bachelor's degree was $65,625 per year. For individual disciplines, the salary difference varied. For example, radiographers with an associate degree averaged $50,800 per year, while their peers with a baccalaureate averaged $55,063 a year. For bone densitometrists, the average wages were $54,280 a year with an associate degree and $61,893 a year with a bachelor's degree.
