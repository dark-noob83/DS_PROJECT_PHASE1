The terms “objectivity” and “subjectivity,” in their modern usage, generally relate to a perceiving subject (normally a person) and a perceived or unperceived object. The object is something that presumably exists independent of the subject’s perception of it.
Subjectivity. In qualitative research, subjectivity refers to the ways that research is shaped by the particular perspectives, interests, and biography of the researcher. When subjectivity is seen to be acting in such a way that it invalidates the findings of the research, it is often termed bias..
