The base currency of the United States is the U.S. dollar. It is printed in bills in seven denominations : $1, $2, $5, $10, $20, $50, and $100. Previously there have also been five larger denominations: $500, $1,000, $5,000, and $10,000 were printed for general use, and a $100,000 bill for certain internal transactions. 1  1 Overview and history. Overview and history [edit]. High-denomination currency (i.e., banknotes with a negotiable face value of $500 or higher) had been used in the United States since the late 18th century. The first $500 note was issued by the Province of North Carolina, authorized by legislation dated May 10, 1780.
