National Average. According to May 2011 data from the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, postal service mail carriers earned an average yearly salarly of $51,390. The bottom 10 percent of carriers earned an average annual salary of $40,470, while the top 10 percent earn an average of $56,720. The National Association of Letter Carriers states that it takes a carrier about 12 years and six months to reach the highest salary levels.
The United States Postal Service (USPS), also known as the Post Office and U.S. Mail, is an independent agency of the United States government responsible for providing postal service in the United States. It is one of the few government agencies explicitly authorized by the United States Constitution.
