1 A high-pitched whistle used to train dogs, typically having a sound inaudible to humans. Example sentences. 1  He kept blowing a dog whistle in a fruitless attempt to coax Molly out. 2  Similar in concept to a dog whistle, it alters the report to an ultrasonic pitch the human ear cannot detect.
The analogy is to a dog whistle, whose high-frequency whistle is heard by dogs but inaudible to humans. The term can be distinguished from  code words  used in some specialist professions, in that dog-whistling is specific to the political realm. The messaging referred to as the dog-whistle has an understandable meaning for a general audience, rather than being incomprehensible.
Top Definition. dog whistle. Dog whistle is a type of strategy of communication that sends a message that the general population will take a certain meaning from, but a certain group that is in the know will take away the secret, intended message. Often involves code words.
A physical dog whistle is almost ultrasonic, and so silent to most humans. Dog-whistle politics is political messaging employing coded language that appears to mean one thing to the general population but has an additional, different or more specific resonance for a targeted subgroup.
