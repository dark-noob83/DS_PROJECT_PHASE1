Lymphadenitis. Lymphadenitis is the medical term for an infection in one or more lymph nodes. Lymph nodes are filled with white blood cells that help your body fight off infections. When lymph nodes become infected, it's usually because an infection started somewhere else in your body. A normal lymph node is small and firm. When lymph nodes become infected, they usually increase in size and may be felt in other areas of your body during a physical examination.
