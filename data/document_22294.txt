Indigestion (also known as dyspepsia) happens to almost everyone from time to time. Eating habits or a chronic digestive problem can trigger indigestion. Indigestion can cause stomach pain or bloating. You may also have heartburn, nausea, and vomiting. Other common symptoms of indigestion include: 1  feeling full during a meal and not being able to finish eating. 2  feeling extremely full after eating a normal-sized meal. Common digestive diseases that can cause indigestion include: 1  acid reflux disease (also called GERD). 2  gastric cancer. 3  pancreatic or bile duct abnormalities. 4  peptic ulcers (sores in the lining of the stomach and esophagus caused by the H. pylori bacteria).
