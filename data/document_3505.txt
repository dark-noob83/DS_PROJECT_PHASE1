Many medicines can cause acute kidney injury (acute renal failure), such as: 1  Antibiotics. 2  These include aminoglycosides, cephalosporins, amphotericin B, bacitracin, and vancomycin. 3  Some blood pressure medicines. 4  One example is ACE inhibitors, such as lisinopril and ramipril. 5  Another is angiotensin receptor blockers.
Kidney failure may occur from an acute situation that injures the kidneys or from chronic diseases that gradually cause the kidneys to stop functioning. In acute renal failure, kidney function is lost rapidly and can occur from a variety of insults to the body.
Most street drugs, including heroin, cocaine and ecstasy can cause high blood pressure, stroke, heart failure and even death, in some cases from only one use. Cocaine, heroin and amphetamines also can cause kidney damage.

Our kidneys have great compensatory ability, but they are also very fragile organ. Many factors can cause kidney damages and lead to kidney disease or even kidney failure such as drugs, injuries, systemic diseases, etc. Since the kidneys have the important function to filter blood and discharge wasteful and toxic substances from the blood.
