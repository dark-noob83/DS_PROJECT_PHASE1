Traditionally, GPA is calculated on an unweighted scale. Unweighted GPA is measured on a scale of 0 to 4.0 and doesn’t take into account the difficulty of a student’s coursework. An unweighted GPA represents an A as a 4.0 whether it was earned in an AP or Honors class or a lower level class.
How to Convert Your GPA to a 4.0 Scale. Colleges report GPA (grade point average) on a 4.0 scale. The top grade is an A, which equals 4.0. This is the standard scale at most colleges, and many high schools use it. To convert your GPA to a 4.0 scale: Letter Grade. Percent Grade.
