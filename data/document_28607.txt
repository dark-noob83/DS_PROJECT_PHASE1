Breeding bald eagles typically lay one to three eggs once a year, and they hatch after about 35 days of incubation. Bald Eagles are monogamous and mate for life. Together they build their large nest, called an eyry, at the top of a tall, sturdy tree or they may also nest in a cliff or on the ground in treeless regions.
