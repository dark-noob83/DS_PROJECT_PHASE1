The answer is no. Levalbuterol is the active ingredient in Xopenex HFA but is not a generic version of the medication. Oftentimes, the active ingredient of any drug is referred to as the generic name.. The generic name is different from a generic version of a medicine.
Xopenex HFA is manufactured by Sepracor, Inc. No generic versions of Xopenex HFA are available in the United States. Yet, if you search the Internet for  generic Xopenex HFA, you may find a number of companies selling it. The fact is that these medicines are fake, substandard, and potentially dangerous.

A Stacy Wiegman, PharmD, Pharmacy, answered. Yes, there is a generic version of Xopenex. Brand name Xopenex for nebulization comes in three different strengths and Xopenex HFA inhaler comes in one strength. The generic is available only for the solution for nebulization in the highest strength. Other strengths of the generic may begin to be available after 2012 or 2013.
