Tuition for University of Hartford is $32,758 for the 2014/2015 academic year. This is 30% more expensive than the national average private non-profit four year college tuition of $25,240. The cost is $10,018 and 44% more expensive than the average Connecticut tuition of $22,740 for 4 year colleges. Tuition ranks 21st in Connecticut amongst 4 year colleges for affordability and is the 10th most expensive 4 year college in the state. Price does not vary by residence. 98% of University of Hartford students received grant aid in 2013/2014. The average total aid amount was $17,350. 32 percent of students received aid in the form of Pell Grants from the U.S. Federal Government. The average Pell Grant awarded for 2013/2014 was $4,042.
The University of Hartford (UHart) is a private, independent, nonsectarian, coeducational university located in West Hartford, Connecticut. Its 350-acre (1.4 km 2) main campus touches portions of three municipalities: Bloomfield, Hartford, and West Hartford. The acceptance rate to the University of Hartford in the fall of 2012 was 41% for all new full-time freshman, transfers, re-admits, and fresh starts. Of the admitted students, the majority attend the College of Arts and Sciences.
The annual total list price cost to go to UHart was $48,080 for the 2014/2015 academic year. The cost is the same for all students regardless of Connecticut residence status as there is no in-state discount. 98% of University of Hartford students received grant aid in 2013/2014. The average total aid amount was $17,350. 32 percent of students received aid in the form of Pell Grants from the U.S. Federal Government. The average Pell Grant awarded for 2013/2014 was $4,042.
University of Hartford offers students both housing and dining options. The on-campus housing cost for a typical student was $7,328 in 2013 - 2014 and the cost for a typical meal plan was $4,920. On-campus housing and dining at University of Hartford has changed an average of 3.0% for each of the past five years. If current growth trends in room, board and other expenses continue, we expect this year's incoming freshmen to pay $15,556 their first year of college.
