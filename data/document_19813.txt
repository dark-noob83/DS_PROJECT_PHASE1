India is home to extraordinary types of Indian climate, ranging from tropical in the south to temperate and alpine in the Himalayan north, where eminent regions receive sustained winter snowfall. The climate of India is strongly influenced by the Himalayas and the Thar Desert.
Mountain climate. Types of Indian climate are varied due to India's unique geography and geology that strongly influence its climate. This is particularly true of the Himalayan Mountain in the north and the Thar Desert in the northwest. The Himalayas act as a barrier to the frigid katabolic winds coming down from Central Asia.
