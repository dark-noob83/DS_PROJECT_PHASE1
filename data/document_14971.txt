The Stamp Act. When Parliament passed the Stamp Act in March 1765, things changed. It was the first direct tax on the American colonies. Every legal document had to be written on specially stamped paper, showing proof of payment.
In effect, they were protesting “taxation without representation”! Opposition to the Stamp Act was so strong that Parliament eventually voted to repeal, or cancel, the act on March 18, 1766. For the moment, the colonists had won an important victory in the battle over England’s taxation polices.
The Stamp Act of 1765 was not the first attempt to tax the American colonies. Parliament had passed the Sugar Act and Currency Act the previous year. Because tax was collected at ports though, it was easily circumvented.
