Symptoms of urinary bladder infection in kids include: 1  pain or burning with urination, 2  frequent urination, 3  urgency-a child will have a very strong, painful urge to urinate, 4  fever-mild infections often will cause fever, but moderate to severe bladder infections that involve the kidneys do cause fever. 5  foul-smelling urine.
Look at your urine to identify the symptoms of a bladder infection. If an infection is present, urine will appear dark amber, cloudy and may have a foul odor. You may feel abdominal pain and pressure. Urinating may be difficult; only small amounts of urine may be released at a time.
Antibiotics are the most common treatment for chronic bladder infections. If your symptoms are mild, your GP might ask you to wait for the results of a urine test. This way, your doctor can prescribe the most suitable antibiotic for fighting the bacteria which are causing your infection.
