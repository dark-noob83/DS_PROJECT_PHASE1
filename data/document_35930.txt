Annual Total Cost. The annual total list price cost to go to Pepperdine University was $61,582 for the 2014/2015 academic year. The cost is the same for all students regardless of California residence status as there is no in-state discount.
Summary. At Pepperdine University, 55.9 percent of full-time undergraduates receive some kind of need-based financial aid and the average need-based scholarship or grant award is $37,293. Paying for college doesn't have to be difficult or devastating.
Tuition and Financial Aid. Pepperdine University charges tuition based on per-unit pricing. Tuition for the 2012-2013 academic year for the fully-employed MBA program is $1,476. Pepperdine lists the base program cost for a two year completion of the MBA program at approximately $76,752.
Full-time undergraduate students at Pepperdine University paid $44,902 in tuition and fees for the 2013 - 2014 school year, prior to adjustments for financial need. Of this amount, the cost of tuition totaled $44,650 and the remaining $252 represents additional fees.
A student's total financial aid awards cannot exceed the Cost of Attendance (estimated costs for the academic year). For example, a student with tuition and fees of $10,000 and a living expense budget of $31,134 will have a total Cost of Attendance of $41,134.
Tuition for Pepperdine University is $46,440 for the 2014/2015 academic year. This is 84% more expensive than the national average private non-profit four year college tuition of $25,229. Net Price. The net out-of-pocket total cost you end up paying or financing though student loans is known as the net price. The reported Pepperdine University net price for in-state students $23,773* for the 2013/2014 academic year.
The overall cost for on-campus students to attend Pepperdine University in 2013-2014 was $61,192. To calculate future costs, we looked at the prior five years of data and determined that this cost increased an average of 4.0% each year.
Pepperdine University has a total undergraduate enrollment of 3,451, with a gender distribution of 41 percent male students and 59 percent female students. At this school, 54 percent of the students live in college-owned, -operated, or-affiliated housing and 46 percent of students live off campus.
