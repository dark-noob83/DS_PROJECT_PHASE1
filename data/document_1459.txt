Vitamin A. Vitamin A is important for babies and young children, and some may not be getting enough. It strengthens their immune system, can help their vision in dim light, and maintains healthy skin. Good sources of vitamin A include: 1  dairy products. 2  fortified fat spreads. 3  carrots, sweet potatoes, swede and mangoes.

Vitamins and minerals are important elements of the total nutritional require­ments of your child. Because the human body itself is unable to produce ade­quate amounts of many vitamins, they must be obtained from the diet.
Some children get all the vitamins they need from a healthy balanced diet containing protein from meat, suitable dairy products, fruit, vegetables and wholegrains.
