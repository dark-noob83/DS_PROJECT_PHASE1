Medical Definition of PULSUS ALTERNANS. : alternation of strong and weak beats of the arterial pulse due to alternate strong and weak ventricular contractions.

Pulsus alternans is a physical finding characterized by a regular alternation of the force of the arterial pulse. It almost invariably indicates the presence of severe left ventricular systolic dysfunction.
Mechanical alternans (pulsus alternans) is a condition in which there is a beat-to-beat oscillation in the strength of cardiac muscle contraction at a constant heart rate.
