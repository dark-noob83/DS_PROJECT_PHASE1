ASD varies widely in severity and symptoms and may go unrecognized, especially in mildly affected children or when it is masked by more debilitating handicaps. Very early indicators that require evaluation by an expert include: 1  no babbling or pointing by age 1. 2  no single words by 16 months or two-word phrases by age 2.
