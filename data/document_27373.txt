The average salary for a lead pastor in a megachurch is $147,000, according to a recent survey. Salaries for lead pastors go as high as $400,000 to as low as $40,000, Leadership Network reported in its 2010 Large Church Salary and Benefits Report. Executive pastors at churches that have a weekend attendance of 2,000 or more persons earn, on average, $99,000 a year and worship pastors get paid $75,000.
Pastor salaries, as are teacher salaries, are not keeping up with others with professional degrees, with a minimum of a Bachelor’s degree. According to the National Association of Church Business Administration, the average pastor in 2012 received an annual salary of $28,000.
Pastor average salary is $35,754, median salary is $34,200 with a salary range from $20,000 to $85,000. Pastor salaries are collected from government agencies and companies. Each salary is associated with a real job position.
The average pastor’s salary of $28,000 a year is close to that of a teachers’ average salary in the United States which is $42,000 a year. As I stated before, these are the two lowest paid professionals with degrees in the nation.
Average Pastor Salaries. The average salary for pastor jobs is $25,000. Average pastor salaries can vary greatly due to company, location, industry, experience and benefits. This salary was calculated using the average salary for all jobs with the term pastor anywhere in the job listing.
In a salary survey of large churches undertaken by Leadership Network, with 253 churches responding, lead pastor salaries ranged from $40,000 to $400,000. The average salary for lead pastors of churches with 1,000 to 1,999 worshipers was $110,000.
