Salary Outlook. Administrative assistants earned a median salary of $32,840 in 2013, according to the BLS. The best-paid made approximately $49,370 a year, while the worst-paid made about $20,370.
For example the median expected annual pay for a typical Financial Assistant (Corporate) in the United States is $50,262, so 50% of the people who perform the job of Financial Assistant (Corporate) in the United States are expected to make less than $50,262. Source: HR Reported data as of October 2015. 1  Salary.
The average hourly pay for all administrative assistant roles is around $20 per hour, but will vary by experience and location. Skill level is the biggest differentiator in pay. Those in secretarial and office support roles usually make around $13 per hour, whereas executive assistants can make upwards of $30 per hour.
In 2013, executive assistants earned a median salary of $51,870, according to the BLS. The highest earners made about $74,970, while the lowest earners took home approximately $32,090. The highest-paid executive assistants worked in the metropolitan areas of San Francisco; Nassau, New York; and New York City.
Accounting assistants got paid an average $36,120 per year, or $17.37 per hour, as of May 2011, states the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics. Their annual pay ranged from $21,450 to $53,250, which was equivalent to $10.31 to $25.60 per hour.
Accounting, tax preparation, bookkeeping and payroll services provided the most employment of any business for accounting assistants. About 6 percent of the total 1,643,470 assistants workedin these businesses to make a mean $35,630 per year, or $17.13 per hour.
For instance, according to the BLS, accounting assistants working for accounting firms should make somewhere in the neighborhood of $16.71 per hour, or $34,760 per year, which was the average rate of pay for those in this industry in 2010.
