Delirium may be caused by many things. One of the most common causes of delirium is medications, especially a reaction to a new medication. Among the most common medications that cause delirium are narcotics, benzodiazepines, anti-cholinergic medications, anti-Parkinsonian drugs, and some anti-epileptic medications.
Dementia is the progressive decline of memory and other thinking skills due to the gradual dysfunction and loss of brain cells. The most common cause of dementia is Alzheimer's disease. Some differences between the symptoms of delirium and dementia include: 1  Onset. 2  The onset of delirium occurs within a short time, while dementia usually begins with relatively minor symptoms that gradually worsen over time. 3  Attention.

A person with delirium may experience confusion, disorientation, an inability to focus, memory problems, or changes in perception. These can include hallucinations or experiencing events that aren’t really happening.
Mental illnesses are among the most common causes of hallucinations. Schizophrenia, dementia, and delirium are a few examples. Substance abuse is another fairly common cause. Some people see or hear things that aren’t there after drinking too much alcohol or taking drugs like cocaine or PCP.
