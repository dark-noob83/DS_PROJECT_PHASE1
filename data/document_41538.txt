The national average for a new shower installation project, when performed by a capable contractor, is $2,689. At the low-end of the normal cost range, homeowners are paying $1,109, and at the high end of the average range they’re spending $2,459. More luxurious showers can cost in excess of $10,000 to install. A relatively affordable pump by itself is likely to cost $2,500 minimum. Having a qualified plumber tackle the task of installing it will add at least $350 in labor costs and more if the installation process proves to be complicated or drawn out. Shower pumps are generally not seen in the United States.
At publication, the cost of shower fixtures and controls can range from $20 to $30 for a simple single-handle shower head and faucet to several hundred dollars for a spa shower with several shower heads and electronic controls. Installing multiple shower fixtures may also increase your plumbing costs. Shower enclosures can be constructed of tile, stone, glass or other water resistant material. Prefabricated fiberglass enclosures allow installation of the new shower stall in a single step. At publication, the average cost to install a basic prefabricated shower will run $495.
