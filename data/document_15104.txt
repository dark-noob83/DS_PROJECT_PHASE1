Strong opioids (sometimes called opiates) are medicines used to treat severe pain. Tramadol is a type of strong opioid. It works on your nervous system and brain to reduce the way you feel pain.
Tramadol is sometimes prescribed off-label to treat migraines and drug withdrawal. Some individuals may experience hives as part of an allergic reaction to Tramadol. Drowsiness is a common side effect of Tramadol. Headaches can be a side effect of taking tramadol.
Tramadol is used to relieve moderate to moderately severe pain. Tramadol extended-release tablets and capsules are only used by people who are expected to need medication to relieve pain around-the-clock. Tramadol is in a class of medications called opiate (narcotic) analgesics.
Tramadol, like other narcotics used for the treatment of pain, may be abused.
