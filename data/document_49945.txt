An absolute neutrophil count (ANC) of 500 or more for 3 days in a row is a sign of engraftment. Patients who receive cord blood may need more time to engraft (typically about 25 days but sometimes as long as 42 days). Day 0 to engraftment. Engraftment is when transplanted stem cells enter the bloodstream, make their way to the bone marrow and start making new blood cells. Engraftment occurs 10 days to 4 weeks after a stem cell transplant. The time between when the conditioning therapy is given and engraftment takes place is very important. During this time, recipients can feel tired and generally unwell
