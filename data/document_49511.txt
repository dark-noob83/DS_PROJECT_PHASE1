The climate of Alaska is determined by average temperatures and precipitation received statewide over many years. Precipitation [edit]. Juneau averages over 50 inches (1,270 mm) of precipitation a year, while some other areas in southeast Alaska receive as much as 275 inches (6,980 mm). Average monthly precipitation generally peaks in September or October, and is lowest in May and June.
