papyrus. 1  volume-Originally the name of a scroll or roll of papyrus, from Latin volvere, to roll up.. 2  bible-Derived from biblios, the name for the papyrus produced in the ancient Phoenician city of Byblos. 3  schedule-Goes back to Greek skhede, for leaf of papyrus; it started out meaning a ticket or a brief note.
Papyrus. Papyrus is a thick paper-like material produced from the pith of the papyrus plant, Cyperus papyrus, a wetland sedge that was once abundant in the Sudd of Southern Sudan along with the Nile Delta of Egypt.

Noun. 1. papyrus-paper made from the papyrus plant by cutting it in strips and pressing it flat; used by ancient Egyptians and Greeks and Romans. paper-a material made of cellulose pulp derived mainly from wood or rags or certain grasses.
Full Definition of PAPYRUS. 1. : a tall perennial sedge (Cyperus papyrus) of the Nile valley. 2. : the pith of the papyrus plant especially when made into strips and pressed into a material to write on.
