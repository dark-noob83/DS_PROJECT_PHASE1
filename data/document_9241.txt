Definition of INEFFICACY. : lack of power to produce a desired effect. ADVERTISEMENT.
See In-not, and Efficacy.] Lack of power to produce the desired or proper effect; inefficiency; ineffectualness; futility; uselessness; fruitlessness; as, the inefficacy of medicines or means. [1913 Webster] The seeming inefficacy of censures. --Bp. Hall.
1. efficacy-capacity or power to produce a desired effect; concern about the safety and efficacy of the vaccine. efficaciousness. effectiveness, effectivity, effectuality, effectualness-power to be effective; the quality of being able to bring about an effect. inefficaciousness, inefficacy-a lack of efficacy.
inefficacy. The condition or state of being incapable of accomplishing or effecting anything: helplessness, impotence, inadequacy, incapability, ineffectiveness, ineffectuality, ineffectualness, powerlessness, uselessness. Translations.
