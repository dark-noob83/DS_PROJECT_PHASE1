What is considered the normal weight for a newborn? The normal weight of a baby who reaches full term between 37 and 40 weeks is 2.7–4.1kg (6 – 9 lbs), with an average weight of 3.5kg (7.7 lbs). A baby who weighs less than 2.5kg (5.5 lbs) is considered to have a low birth weight.
Those born before 37 completed weeks of pregnancy are considered premature or late preterm. A late preterm infant is a premature baby born between 34 and 36 weeks gestational age. This is relatively close to full term, which is 37 weeks or greater (Engle, 2007).

A term birth is considered 37 to 41 weeks, but the new study suggests it is more of a continuum. The findings appear in Obstetrics & Gynecology.
