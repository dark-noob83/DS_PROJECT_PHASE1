Understanding Superannuation – Fact Sheet 4 Government co-contributions It has been announced that if you make a non-concessional superannuation contribution of $1,000, the government may contribute up to $500 (2012/13) into your super account. For those under age 65, the bring forward provision allows total contributions of $450,000 over three years. For persons aged 65 to 75, a contribution limit of $150,000 per annum is allowed, providing they work at least 40 hours in 30 consecutive days in the financial year of contribution.
Superannuation in Australia refers to the arrangements people make in Australia to accrue funds to replace their income in retirement. Superannuation in Australia is government-supported and encouraged, and minimum provisions are compulsory for employees. Since 1 July 2003, the Government has made available incentives of a Government co-contribution of up to $1,500 for lower income employees who make personal contributions to their own superannuation fund. Depending on individual income thresholds, the Government pays up to $1.50 for every $1 contributed.
