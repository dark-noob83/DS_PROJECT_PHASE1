Prakrit Language Classes at Oxford. Manuscript of the 'Uttaradhyayana Sutra', written in Prakrit prose and verse in the 16th century. The term 'Prakrit' is commonly translated as 'natural' and refers to a variety of languages and dialects originally used in India.
The noun PRAKRIT has 2 senses: 1. any of the modern Indic languages. 2. any of the vernacular Indic languages of north and central India (as distinguished from Sanskrit) recorded from the 3rd century BC to the 4th century AD. Familiarity information: PRAKRIT used as a noun is rare.
