Gout is a form of inflammatory arthritis that develops in some people who have high levels of uric acid in the blood. It occurs in about 4 percent of American adults, but is more likely to affect men than women.
Symptoms of Acute Gouty Arthritis. Acute gouty arthritis occurs when the first symptoms of gout appear. Sometimes the first signs of gout are brief twinges of pain (petit attacks) in an affected joint. These attacks can precede the actual full-blown condition by several years. Symptoms of acute gouty arthritis include: 1  Severe pain at and around the joint. 2  May feel like crushing or a dislocated bone. 3  Physical activity and even the weight of bed sheets may be unbearable.
