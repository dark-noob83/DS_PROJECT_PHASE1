The country’s 55,410 veterinarians made an average $91,250 per year as of May 2011, according to the BLS. However, annual pay ranged from $50,480 to $141,680 per year. Veterinarians diagnose and treat pets and animals on farms, ranches, zoos, racetracks and laboratories.
A veterinarian earns an average annual salary of $56,685 to $80,906, according to 2011 Payscale.com data. With the inclusion of commissions, profit sharing and bonuses, veterinarians can earn total incomes of $68,486 to $122,199 per year. Veterinarians' annual salaries will generally increase with experience. For example, a veterinarian with less than one year of experience earns an annual salary of $50,621 to $68,968.
The average annual salary for veterinarians in 2013 was $96,140, according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, with a range from $53,270 to $149,530. Veterinarians in private practice – the most common work setting – took home $96,510 on average.
