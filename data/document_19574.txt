Only the wealthiest estates pay the tax because it is levied only on the portion of an estate's value that exceeds a specified exemption level -- $5.43 million per person (effectively $10.86 million per married couple) in 2015.
The federal estate tax is a tax on property (cash, real estate, stock, or other assets) transferred from deceased persons to their heirs.
Knowing your potential estate tax liability is a great place to start your estate tax plan. Use this calculator to project the value of your estate, and the associated estate tax, for the next ten years. For 2015, the maximum estate tax is 40% with the first $5.43 million of an estate not subject to the tax.
Exemption Amounts. In 2015, every person may leave or give away up to $5.43 million without owing any estate tax. As a practical matter, that means that under the new rules about 99.5% of all estates will NOT owe any federal gift/estate tax. The exemption amount is indexed for inflation each year.
For 2011, the federal estate tax exemption will be $5 million and the estate tax rate for estates valued over this amount will be 35%.
Estate Tax. The Estate Tax is a tax on your right to transfer property at your death. It consists of an accounting of everything you own or have certain interests in at the date of death (Refer to Form 706 (PDF)).
For the 2014 tax year, the estate tax exclusion amount is $5.34 million. It increases to $5.43 million for 2015. ATRA also increased the tax rate on estates in excess of the exemption amount from 35 percent to 40 percent. The new estate tax law, however, does offer permanent portability between spouses.
