The Jamia Millia Islamia (JMI) is a central University based in New Delhi, India. Originally, the University was established in 1920 in Aligarh, United Province, now, Uttar Pradesh (UP) . Later, because of political disturbances, the University was shifted to Karol Bagh in New Delhi.
Jamia Millia Islamia is a public central university located in Delhi. It was established at Aligarh in United Provinces, India in 1920. It became a Central University by an act of the Indian Parliament in 1988. In Urdu and Arabic, Jamia means University, and Millia means National.

The foundation stone of new campus in South Delhi was laid on March 1, 1935. The University Grants Commission (UGC) of India accorded the Deemed to be University status on Jamia Millia Islamia in 1962. By a Special Act of the Parliament, Jamia Millia Islamia was made a central University of India in December 1988.
