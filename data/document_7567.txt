Hastings Name Meaning. English and Scottish: habitational name from Hastings, a place in Sussex, on the south coast of England, near which the English army was defeated by the Normans in 1066.
Hastings has its origins in the Old English language and it is used largely in English. Hastings is of the meaning 'from Hastings, England'. This is also the name of the city of Hastings in East Sussex, England. It is famous from the Battle of Hastings (1066), which resulted in the Norman conquest of England.
