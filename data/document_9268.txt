Salaried Nurse-Midwives. Midwives who are salaried employees of a hospital or practice earn annual incomes which vary considerably. Based on a survey taken in 2007, the American College of Nurse-Midwives reports that the median salary of a nurse-midwife who works 35 hours or more per week is between $79,093 and $89,916.
Five states topped the list for CRNM average hourly wages in 2013, according to the BLS. Oregon CRNMs earned $50.72 an hour, while CRNMs in Massachusetts earned $51.33 an hour. In North Dakota, CRNMs earned an average hourly wage of $51.55.
The average annual salary of a certified nurse midwife is $91,070. The highest paid nurse midwives earn around $119,690, while the lowest paid come in around $62,480 per year. The average hourly salary for certified nurse midwives ranges from $30.04 to $57.55 with the median at $43.08 per hour. As a nurse midwife, you may enjoy an annual bonus of up to $29,799. Indiana, New York, Texas, California, and Florida are the states that employ the highest number of certified nurse midwives.
Average nurse midwife salary. According to BLS, the average salary for nurse midwife in the USA is $89,600 per year while the hourly rate for nurse midwife is from $30.04 to $57.55.
