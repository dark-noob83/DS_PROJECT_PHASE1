Corpus Christi Weather In November. The highest recorded temperature for Corpus Christi for the past 72 years in November was 98 degrees Fahrenheit, most recently registered on November 7, 1988, and the standard high is 75.
Corpus Christi, Texas has a warm humid temperate climate with hot summers and no dry season. The area within 25 mi of this station is covered by grasslands (59%), croplands (16%), oceans and seas (15%), lakes and rivers (4%), and forests (3%).
The average daily low temperature in Corpus Christi on November First is 59 degrees Fahrenheit and the average low on the last day of November is 50 degrees. Corpus Christi usually receives an average of 1.74 inches of precipitation during November.
The coldest average temperature in Corpus Christi is January at 58.3 degrees. The most monthly precipitation in Corpus Christi occurs in July with 5 inches. The Corpus Christi weather information is based on the average of the previous 3-7 years of data.
Rain, Rain, Gone Away. November may not be the warmest month in Corpus Christi -- average daytime temperatures reach the mid 90s in August -- but it's certainly one of the driest. November boasts the third lowest rainfall total after March and December, or less than 2 inches per month, on average.
Despite an average of only six hours of sunshine per day, November is a relatively warm month for Corpus Christi. Average temperatures reach the high 70s during the day and dip down to the high 50s at night, making for a comfortable, no-heat-needed month in this southerly city.
