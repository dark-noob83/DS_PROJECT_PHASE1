Sesame seed is considered to be the oldest oilseed crop known, domesticated well over 5000 years ago. Sesame seeds come in many colours, the most traded variety of sesame is off-white coloured. Other common colours are buff, tan, gold, brown, reddish, gray and black. History. Sesame seeds history as a medicine goes back 3600 years to Egyptian times where it was listed in the scrolls of the Ebers as a favoured medicine.
Sesame (/ˈsɛsəmiː/ ; Sesamum indicum) is a flowering plant in the genus Sesamum. Numerous wild relatives occur in Africa and a smaller number in India. It is widely naturalized in tropical regions around the world and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which grow in pods.
