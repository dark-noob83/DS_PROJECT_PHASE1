cay. n. 1. (Physical Geography) a small low island or bank composed of sand and coral fragments, esp in the Caribbean area. Also called: key. [C18: from Spanish cayo, probably from Old French quai quay].
Cay. A cay, also spelled caye or key, is a small, low-elevation, sandy island formed on the surface of a coral reef. Cays occur in tropical environments throughout the Pacific, Atlantic and Indian Oceans, where they can provide habitable and agricultural land for hundreds of thousands of people.
