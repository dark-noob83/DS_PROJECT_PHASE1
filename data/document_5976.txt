Crushed stone is not a standard commodity.. It is made by mining one of several types of rock such as limestone, granite, trap rock, scoria, basalt, dolomite, or sandstone; crushing the rock; and, then screening the crushed rock to sizes that are suitable for the intended end use. Crushed stone is the world's most basic mineral commodity. Crushed stone is a bulk commodity that is very heavy and very costly to handle and transport. In 2012, the average cost of crushed stone in the United States at the plant site was $9.75 per metric ton. Transportation to the job site can significantly increase the cost of the stone.
