This famous name is of Anglo-Saxon origin, and is a locational surname deriving from any one of the numerous places called Burton in England, found mainly in the midland and northern counties.
Some of the places mean settlement belonging to a fort, from the same Olde English elements, while Burton in Somerset means the settlement on the River Bredy or Bride, and Burton in Sussex translates as Budeca's settlement.
Burton Early Origins. First found in Shropshire where they were descended conjecturally from Drogo de Beuvriere a kinsman of William the Conqueror who held lands at Burton Agnes, Burton Constable and a manor house or castle at Burton Pidsea.
