Average Annual Temperature. The African savanna has a relatively moderate temperature with it not going over 86° F and not going under 68° F. During the winter (which can pretty much be classified as the dry season) the temperature will range from about 68° F - 78° F (or 20° C - 25° C).
Annual Rainfall. Nearly all of the precipitation in the African Savanna occurs during the wet season. During the wet season, which stretches from May to November, the savanna receives a total of around 100 inches of rain or about 20 inches per month (254 cm per dry season and 50.8 per month). During the dry season, there is a total of around only 4 inches throughout the entire season (or 10.16 cm).
The average precipitation per year in the Australian Savanna is around 20 to 40 in. In both the Australian, and the African savanna, the average precipitation in the dry season is about 4 in. The average precipitation in the Australian and the Africa savanna during the wet season is around 15 to 25 inches.
It is usually cooler during the dry season by a few degrees. Because it is in the tropical latitudes that is still hot enough.
