Patmos. Patmos is a small Greek island in the Aegean Sea. One of the northernmost islands of the Dodecanese complex, it has a population of 2,998 and an area of 34.05 km². The highest point is Profitis Ilias, 269 metres above sea level.

The Isle of Patmos is a volcanic island in the Icarian Sea (Aegean) near Asia Minor. Because it was SO isolated, barren, and rocky, it was used as a 'penal isle' in John's day … , when Domitian ordered him imprisoned there. Today wheat, grapes and olives are cultivated in the rich volcanic soil.
