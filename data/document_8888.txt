Leatherback turtles are named for their shell, which is leather-like rather than hard, like other turtles. They are the largest sea turtle species and also one of the most migratory, crossing both the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
The leatherback sea turtle can grow to 6.5 feet long and weigh up to 1,400 pounds, making it the largest of the sea turtles. It can survive in most of the world's oceans, but returns to tropical beaches to lay its eggs.

Worldwide, six of the seven sea turtle species are classified as threatened or endangered due to human actions and lifestyles.
