Family doctors’ annual pay can vary dramatically depending on location. Family physicians based in the North Central region earn the highest salaries — $186,000 a year on average – followed by those employed in the South Central region, who earn around $182,000 a year according to Medscape’s salary data.
A family physician’s salary varies based on the age such as the one in 20s earns up to £108,750, in 30s earns about £142,100, in 40s earns about £168,200 and in 50s earns about £168,200. The average hourly wage for a family physician in UK is £72.5.
An average salary for a family physician based in Canada ranges from between 56,526 CAD – 137,027 CAD per annum while an average hourly wage ranges from between 32 CAD -78 CAD per hour.
The median hourly wage for a family physician is about $81.03 per hour. The lowest 10 percent of family physicians earn about $39.73 per hour while the upper 10 percent of them earn more than $80.00 per hour.
The hourly wage for family doctors depends on a number of factors, including years of experience. Physicians with 1 to 4 years of experience typically earn between $35.75 and $103.76 per hour, and those with 5 to 9 years of experience are generally paid between $63.24 and $96.80 per hour.
