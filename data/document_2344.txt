Hibiscus (/hɨˈbɪskəs/ or /haɪˈbɪskəs/) is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae. It is quite large, containing several hundred species that are native to warm-temperate, subtropical and tropical regions throughout the world.
The scientific name for Hibiscus is Hibiscus rosa-sinensis. Although it is most commonly called Hibiscus, it is also known as rosemallow in some places, according to Gardenology.org.
Scientific Name. Hibiscus is a member of the mallow family Malvaceae in the genus Hibiscus. The word hibiscus (marsh mallow) is derived by way of Latin from the Greek word hibisko. There are over 200 species of hibiscus in the mallow family.

HIBISCUS Overview Information. Hibiscus is a bushy annual plant. Parts of the flower are used to make a popular drink in Egypt called Karkade. Various parts of the plant are also used to make jams, spices, soups, and sauces. The flowers are used to make medicine.
