The carbonic anhydrases (or carbonate dehydratases) form a family of enzymes that catalyze the rapid interconversion of carbon dioxide and water to bicarbonate and protons (or vice versa), a reversible reaction that occurs relatively slowly in the absence of a catalyst. Typical catalytic rates of the different forms of this enzyme ranging between 10 4 and 10 6 reactions per second. The reverse reaction is relatively slow (kinetics in the 15-second range)

In inorganic chemistry, bicarbonate (IUPAC-recommended nomenclature: hydrogen carbonate) is an intermediate form in the deprotonation of carbonic acid. It is a polyatomic anion with the chemical formula H C O 3 − . Bicarbonate serves a crucial biochemical role in the physiological pH buffering system.
