Hand, foot and mouth disease is a viral infection that can affect young children. It doesn't usually pose a serious threat to a child's health, but it can be be an unpleasant condition, particularly if it affects younger children.

It usually takes 3 to 6 days for a person to get symptoms of hand-foot-and-mouth disease after being exposed to the virus. This is called the incubation period.
Hand, foot and mouth disease is a self-limiting condition, which means that it will get better by on its own without treatment. The symptoms will usually pass within seven days.
