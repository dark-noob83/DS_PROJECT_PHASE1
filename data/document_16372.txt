Full Definition of CAPITALISM. : an economic system characterized by private or corporate ownership of capital goods, by investments that are determined by private decision, and by prices, production, and the distribution of goods that are determined mainly by competition in a free market.
A Capitalist economic system is often contrasted to a socialist or communist economic system where economic decisions are made centrally by government agencies.
Capitalism. 1  Capitalism is based on private ownership of the means of production and on individual economic freedom. 2  Most of the means of production, such as factories and businesses, are owned by private individuals and not by the government.
Private property. In a capitalist economy, people have rights to the fruit of their labor and whatever other property they acquire through legal means. Property includes a person's life (we own our own bodies) and liberty as well as physical possessions. Alienable property--possessions--can be sold or leased to others.
A Capitalist economic system is one characterised by free markets and the absence of government intervention in the economy. In practice a capitalist economy will need some government intervention, primarily to protect private property.
Not a Philosophy but an Economy. The word capitalism sounds like the label of a philosophy, rather than a system of producing, distributing, and consuming goods and services. But capitalism is, in fact, just a type of economy.
