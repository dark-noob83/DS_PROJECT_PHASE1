symptom formation. n. The process of developing a physical or behavioral substitute for an unconscious impulse or a conflict that causes anxiety, such as avoiding crowds. Also called symptom substitution.
ABSTRACT. A definition of the term substitution hypothesis is presented. It refers to a theory in psychology that was proposed by psychologist Sigmund Freud. According to this theory, if psychoneurotic symptoms are treated without dealing with the causes of the disease, symptom substitution will occur.
