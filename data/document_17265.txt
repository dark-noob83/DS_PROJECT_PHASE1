The term corned is not a reference to the use of corn in the recipe, but rather the corn-sized granules of salt that were once used to cure the meat. Today, most corned beef is cured in a salt brine rather than a dry salt rub. Corned beef purchased in the brine can be stored in the refrigerator for five to seven days or until the use by date before cooking.
