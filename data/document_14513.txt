Operation Michael was a First World War German military operation that began the Spring Offensive on 21 March 1918. It was launched from the Hindenburg Line, in the vicinity of Saint-Quentin, France.
The first and largest of these offensives, Operation Michael, was intended to strike the British Expeditionary Force (BEF) along the Somme with the goal of cutting it off from the French to the south.
Operation Michael-Conflict & Dates: Operation Michael commenced on March 21, 1918, and was the beginning of the German Spring Offensives during World War I (1914-1918).
