Corniculate and Cuneiform Cartilages. These minor paired cartilages are seen as nodules in the posterior aryepiglottic folds. The corniculate cartilages attach themselves to the apex of the arytenoid cartilages where the aryepiglottic fold inserts and that is how the nodule-shape in the fold is made. The cuneiform cartilages do not attach themselves to any other cartilages, just muscles and ligaments.
The arytenoid cartilages are the only major cartilages to be paired, since the corniculate and cuneiform cartilages are seen to be minor cartilages. They are pyramidal in shape and have three faces.
