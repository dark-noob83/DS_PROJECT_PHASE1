Columbine is a census-designated place (CDP) in Arapahoe and Jefferson Counties in the U.S. state of Colorado. Located mostly in Jefferson County, it lies immediately west of Littleton. The population was 24,280 at the 2010 census. Columbine High School, made infamous by the Columbine High School massacre, is located in the Columbine CDP. The ZIP code for the community is 80123 & 80128.
Columbine-area historical tornado activity is above Colorado state average. It is 16% greater than the overall U.S. average. On 6/15/1988, a category F3 (max. wind speeds 158-206 mph) tornado 12.3 miles away from the Columbine place center injured 7 people and caused between $5,000,000 and $50,000,000 in damages.

Columbine is a recognizable plant to many Colorado gardeners. The familiar blue and white Colorado columbine is our state flower. And there are many kinds of columbine that feature a variety of flower colors and form. 
Colorado specifies the white and lavender Rocky Mt. Columbine which has blue-violet petals and spurs, a white cup and yellow center. Blue is a symbol of the sky, white represents snow, and yellow symbolizes Colorado's gold mining history. Another honor was bestowed on the Rocky Mountain columbine in 1915 when the song Where The Columbines Grow was adopted as the official Colorado state song.
