During the period from mid-1995 to 1997, Lewinsky was involved in a sexual relationship with President Bill Clinton. Monica Samille Lewinsky, born July 23, 1973 in San Francisco, California, United States is an American woman with whom then-United States President Bill Clinton admitted to having had an improper relationship while Lewinsky worked at the White House in 1995 and 1996. The affair and its repercussions, especially the impeachment of Bill Clinton, became known as the Lewinsky scandal. Monica Lewinsky was born in San Francisco, California, and grew up in an affluent family in Southern California in the Westside Brentwood area of Los Angeles and in Beverly Hills. She is of Jewish descent.
Monica's maternal grandfather, Samuel M. Vilensky, was a Lithuanian Jew, and Monica's maternal grandmother, Bronia Poleshuk, was born in the British Concession of Tianjin, China, to a Russian Jewish family. Monica's parents' acrimonious separation and divorce during 1987 and 1988 had a significant effect on her.
