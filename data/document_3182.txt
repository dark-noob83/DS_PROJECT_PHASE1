Three major components of Georgia’s transportation system are vital to the state’s economy-the interstate highway system, Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, and the deepwater ports of Savannah and Brunswick.
Since their construction in the 1960s and 1970s, Atlanta's interstate highways have helped fuel urban sprawl, first around the northern I-285 perimeter and Interstates 75 and 85 north of the city in the 1960s, 1970s, and 1980s.
Spaghetti Junction in Atlanta. Other superhighways with interstate designations are circumferential highways or spurs connecting interstate highways within Georgia.
