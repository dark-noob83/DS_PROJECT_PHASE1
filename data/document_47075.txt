The sternum, commonly known as the breastbone, is a long, narrow flat bone that serves as the keystone of the rib cage and stabilizes the thoracic skeleton. Several muscles that move the arms, head, and neck have their origins on the sternum.
Manubrium. 1  The manubrium sterni is the most superior part of the sternum that lies at the levels of 3rd and 4th thoracic vertebrae. 2  The superior border of the manubrium has the broadest structure among the other parts of the sternum. 3  On its center is the suprasternal or presternal or jugular notch.

The manubrium is a section of the sternum or breastbone in the human chest. The horizontal collar bone connects to the manubrium at the sternum. Injuries to the manubrium may limit range of motion, and cause pain when exercising.
The manubrium is a section of the sternum or breastbone in the human chest. Situated at the top of the bone between the two clavicles or collarbones, it is the broadest portion of the sternum.
