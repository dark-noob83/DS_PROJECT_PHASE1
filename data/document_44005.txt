How much protein you need. Pregnant women: about 70 grams (g) of protein per day. Non-pregnant women: about 45 g. You don't have to get the recommended amount of protein every day. Instead, aim for that amount as an average over the course of a few days or a week. 
But the message the rest of us often get is that we’re eating too much protein. The Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) for protein is a modest 0.8 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight. The RDA is the amount of a nutrient you need to meet your basic nutritional requirements. In a sense, it’s the minimum amount you need to keep from getting sick — not the specific amount you are supposed to eat every day. To determine your RDA for protein, you can multiply your weight in pounds by 0.36, or use this online protein calculator.
