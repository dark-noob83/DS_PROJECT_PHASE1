Dale Earnhardt, Sr. was an outstanding race car driver who spent most of his career driving on the NASCAR circuit. In 1975, Dale began his racing career competing in the World 600 at the Charlotte Motor Speedway in Charlotte, North Carolina, where he finished in 22nd place.
Dale Earnhardt was an American race car driver who gained worldwide fame as a stock car driver for NASCAR, recording seven Winston Cup championship victories and 76 career wins, including the 1998 Daytona 500.
Dale Earnhardt was born on April 29, 1951, in Kannapolis, North Carolina. He drove in NASCAR's top division and was the first driver to win Rookie of the Year and the championship back-to-back.
