Reticence (noun). the quality or state of being reticent, or keeping silence; the state of holding one's tonque; refraining to speak of that which is suggested; uncommunicativeness. Reticence (noun). a figure by which a person really speaks of a thing while he makes a show as if he would say nothingon the subject.
Latin reticent-, reticens, present participle of reticēre to keep silent, from re- + tacēre to be silent — more at tacit.
reticence. 1. The keeping of one's thoughts and emotions to oneself: control, reserve, restraint, self-control, self-restraint, taciturnity, uncommunicativeness. 2.
Reticence is being reserved, quiet or reluctant. An example of reticence is a person sitting in a corner not engaging with anyone at a dinner party. the quality or state, or an instance, of being reticent; reserve. also ret′i·cency.
