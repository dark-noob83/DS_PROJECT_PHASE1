Average Salary & Benefits. Electrical contractors earned an average income of $52,910 per year, according to the BLS. They are usually paid by the hour, and their wages are highly contingent upon experience, industry and geographical area. The middle half made between $37,570 and $65,260 per year.
Salary by State. An electrical contractor's pay can vary significantly by state. They earned the highest incomes of $72,030 in Alaska, according to the BLS. Those in Illinois also earned relatively high incomes, $70,430 annually. Electrical contractors also earned above-average pay of $62,550 per year in California.
Industries. In 2011, nonresidential building construction provided almost a quarter of the 195,000 jobs for contractors, with average salaries at $45.41 per hour, or $94,450 per year. Building equipment contractors contained 14 percent of the positions at a mean $46.52 per hour, or $96,7
The average wage for a General Contractor is $29.74 per hour. Experience has a moderate effect on pay for this job. Skills that are associated with high pay for this job are Sales, Contractor Management, Construction Estimating, and Project Management.
Basics. General contractors earned a mean $45.14 per hour, or $93,900 per year, as of May 2011, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. The lowest-paid 10 percent of contractors received less than $24.35 per hour, or $50,650 per year, while the best-paid 10 percent made $71.67 per hour, or $149,070 per year.
