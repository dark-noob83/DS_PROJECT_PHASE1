For ciprofloxacin tablets, the usual dosage is between 250 mg and 750 mg every 12 hours, depending on the type of infection. For intravenous (IV) ciprofloxacin, the usual dose is between 200 mg and 400 mg every 8 or 12 hours. The usual dosage for ciprofloxacin extended-release tablets is 500 mg to 1000 mg once a day. The length of treatment will vary, depending on the type and severity of the infection. Most people will likely take ciprofloxacin for 7 to 14 days. For ciprofloxacin ear drops the standard dosage is one single-use container in the infected ear twice daily, about 12 hours apart, for seven days. Each single-use container has the correct amount of medicine needed for one dose.
