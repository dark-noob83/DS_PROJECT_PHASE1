Tuition and Fees Discounted Based On Residency. Full-time undergraduate students that do not reside in Rhode Island paid $28,016 in tuition and fees for the 2013 - 2014 school year prior to adjustments for financial need. Of this amount, $26,444 was the cost of tuition and $1,572 the cost of fees.
The Community College of Rhode Island is a state-supported public institution; therefore, tuition and fees are subject to increase at any time by action of the Rhode Island Board of Governors for Higher Education.
Courses. A course fee may be charged for certain undergraduate and graduate courses. Tier I fees are $25, Tier II fees are $55, and Tier III fees are $75 (per course). Undergraduate engineering students pay a program fee of $480 per semester if attending full-time,or $40 per credit if attending part-time.
The cost of tuition and fees is reduced dramatically for residents of Rhode Island. Tuition for these students is set at $10,878 for the 2013 - 2014 year, a discount of 55.6%. Fees are $1,572, bringing the total yearly cost for these full-time undergrads to $12,450.
Future Full-Time Tuition and Fees. Tuition for out-of-state students attending University of Rhode Island increased at an average annual rate of 2.1% over the past 5 years. Over that same period, fees grew by 5.4%. Based on this, we estimate the cost of tuition and fees for 2014 - 2015 will total $28,643.
